Site Survey Methodology

Professional RF site surveys are the foundation of successful wireless deployments. Our multi-phase survey approach ensures designs meet coverage, capacity, and performance requirements.

Survey Type 1: Predictive Site Survey

Purpose:

Pre-deployment RF modeling to determine AP quantities, placement, and preliminary channel plan before equipment procurement.

Activities:

  • Floor Plan Analysis ‚Äì Building blueprints imported into RF planning software, scaled and calibrated
  • Material Assessment ‚Äì Attenuation values assigned to walls, doors, windows, metal studs, concrete (typical: drywall 3 dB, concrete block 8 dB, metal 20+ dB)
  • Requirements Mapping ‚Äì Coverage areas defined, capacity zones identified, performance targets set
  • AP Placement Modeling ‚Äì Virtual AP placement, power levels, antenna patterns, coverage predictions
  • Coverage Heat Maps ‚Äì Signal strength (RSSI), signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), data rate predictions

Tools Used:

  • Ekahau AI Pro ‚Äì Industry-leading WiFi planning and site survey platform
  • AP manufacturer planning tools (Aruba Visual RF, Cisco Prime, Ubiquiti Design Center)

Deliverables:

  • Predictive coverage heat maps (signal strength, SNR, channel overlap)
  • AP placement recommendations with mounting heights
  • Preliminary channel and power plan
  • Equipment bill of materials

Survey Type 2: Passive Site Survey

Purpose:

Measure existing RF environment before deployment or assess current network performance for optimization.

Activities:

  • RF Spectrum Analysis ‚Äì 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz spectrum scanning for interference sources (neighboring WiFi, Bluetooth, microwave ovens, wireless cameras)
  • Signal Strength Mapping ‚Äì Walk survey measuring RSSI from existing or temporary APs
  • Channel Utilization ‚Äì Airtime usage, co-channel interference, adjacent channel interference
  • Rogue AP Detection ‚Äì Unauthorized access points and security threats

Deliverables:

  • Spectrum analysis reports identifying interference sources
  • Coverage heat maps showing signal quality
  • Channel utilization analysis
  • Rogue AP identification and locations

Survey Type 3: Active Site Survey

Purpose:

Post-deployment validation measuring actual network performance from client perspective.

Activities:

  • Association Testing ‚Äì Client device connectivity at various locations
  • Throughput Measurement ‚Äì iPerf tests measuring TCP/UDP throughput, latency, jitter, packet loss
  • Roaming Validation ‚Äì Client handoff behavior between APs, roaming thresholds verification
  • Application Testing ‚Äì Voice (VoIP), video (streaming), data application performance

Performance Criteria:

  • Coverage: Minimum -67 dBm RSSI, -70 dBm for voice applications
  • SNR: Minimum 25 dB (general), 30+ dB (high-density areas)
  • Throughput: >50 Mbps per client (typical), >100 Mbps (WiFi 6 high-performance)
  • Roaming: <50ms handoff time (voice/video applications)

Deliverables:

  • Actual vs. predicted coverage comparison
  • Throughput heat maps
  • Roaming performance analysis
  • Application performance reports
  • Optimization recommendations

RF Design Engineering

Detailed RF engineering ensures optimal coverage, capacity, and performance while minimizing interference and cost.

Coverage Optimization

Design Principles:

  • Signal Strength Targets ‚Äì Minimum -67 dBm for data, -65 dBm for voice, overlap zones at -75 dBm for seamless roaming
  • AP Density ‚Äì Coverage-driven (1 AP per 2,000-5,000 sq ft typical office) vs. capacity-driven (1 AP per 25-50 users high-density)
  • Power Levels ‚Äì Balanced to avoid over-coverage and excessive co-channel interference (typical 8-17 dBm transmit power)
  • Antenna Selection ‚Äì Omnidirectional (typical office), directional (long corridors, outdoor), downtilt for high ceilings

Channel Planning

Frequency Band Strategies:

  • 2.4 GHz ‚Äì 3 non-overlapping channels (1, 6, 11), legacy device support, IoT connectivity, avoid in high-density environments
  • 5 GHz ‚Äì Up to 25 non-overlapping channels (UNII-1, UNII-2, UNII-3), primary band for high-performance clients
  • 6 GHz (WiFi 6E) ‚Äì 59 channels (20 MHz), 29 channels (40 MHz), 14 channels (80 MHz), 7 channels (160 MHz), clean spectrum with no legacy interference

Channel Assignment:

  • Manual Planning ‚Äì Fixed channel assignments for stable environments, reuse pattern design (1-6-11 for 2.4 GHz, every 4th channel for 5 GHz 20 MHz)
  • Dynamic Assignment (RRM) ‚Äì Automatic Radio Resource Management adjusting channels based on interference, suitable for changing environments
  • DFS Channels ‚Äì Utilize Dynamic Frequency Selection channels (52-144) in 5 GHz for additional capacity, radar detection compliance

Capacity Planning

User Density Analysis:

  • Client Count per AP ‚Äì Typical office: 25-30 clients, high-density: 50-75 clients with WiFi 6 OFDMA/MU-MIMO
  • Bandwidth per User ‚Äì Light usage: 1-2 Mbps, typical: 5-10 Mbps, high-definition video: 20-50 Mbps
  • Concurrent Usage ‚Äì Percentage of clients active simultaneously (office 30-50%, conference room 100%, stadium 70-90%)

WiFi 6/6E Optimization:

  • OFDMA ‚Äì Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access for efficient multi-user support, divides channels into resource units (RUs)
  • MU-MIMO ‚Äì Multi-User MIMO supporting 8x8 simultaneous streams (WiFi 6), 16x16 (WiFi 7)
  • Band Steering ‚Äì Client steering from 2.4 GHz to 5/6 GHz for better performance
  • Load Balancing ‚Äì Distributing clients across APs to prevent single AP overload

Network Architecture Design

Controller & Management

Deployment Models:

  • Hardware Controller ‚Äì On-premise appliance, suitable for large enterprises, full local control
  • Virtual Controller ‚Äì VM-based, flexible scaling, integration with virtualized infrastructure
  • Cloud-Managed ‚Äì SaaS platform (Meraki, Aruba Central, UniFi Cloud), simplified management, multi-site visibility

Network Segmentation:

  • SSID Architecture ‚Äì Corporate (WPA3-Enterprise, 802.1X authentication), Guest (isolated, captive portal), IoT (separate VLAN, restricted access), Voice (QoS priority)
  • VLAN Design ‚Äì Per-SSID VLAN assignment, dynamic VLAN based on 802.1X attributes, firewall policies between segments

Specialized WiFi Solutions

Healthcare WiFi Networks

Unique Requirements:

  • Medical Device Connectivity ‚Äì Infusion pumps, patient monitors, wireless EKG, imaging equipment
  • RTLS (Real-Time Location Systems) ‚Äì Asset tracking, patient tracking, staff duress badges
  • Infection Control ‚Äì Sealed APs for cleanroom environments, antimicrobial coatings
  • HIPAA Compliance ‚Äì Encrypted communications, audit logging, secure guest isolation

Design Considerations:

  • Dense AP placement for RTLS accuracy (6-12 ft location precision requires 3-4 APs visibility)
  • Dedicated medical device SSID with QoS priority
  • RF containment preventing signal leakage outside facility

High-Density Venue WiFi

Applications:

  • Convention centers and conference facilities

Design Strategies:

  • High AP Density ‚Äì 1 AP per 50-100 users, directional antennas limiting coverage area
  • Minimize Channel Width ‚Äì 20 MHz channels maximizing channel count in 5 GHz
  • Load Balancing ‚Äì Client steering preventing single AP overload
  • Capacity Planning ‚Äì User behavior analysis (social media, streaming, ticketing apps)

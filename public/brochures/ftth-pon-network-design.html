Engineering Methodology

Our FTTH network design process follows proven workflows optimized for cost-effective deployment and operational efficiency. Each phase produces construction-ready deliverables.

Phase 1: Service Area Analysis & Demand Planning

Activities:

  • Market Assessment ‚Äì Demographic analysis, household income levels, competitive landscape, broadband availability gaps
  • Customer Mapping ‚Äì GIS-based address point geocoding, building footprint analysis, parcel data integration
  • Density Analysis ‚Äì Homes per route mile calculation, cluster identification, service area prioritization
  • Demand Forecasting ‚Äì Take-rate assumptions (typical 40-60% residential, 70-90% MDU), penetration modeling, service tier mix
  • Capacity Planning ‚Äì Bandwidth per subscriber (100 Mbps to multi-gigabit), peak hour traffic modeling, oversubscription ratios (20:1 to 50:1 typical)

Tools Used:

  • QGIS / ArcGIS for geospatial analysis and mapping
  • US Census data, parcel databases, address point datasets
  • Google Earth Pro for preliminary route visualization
  • Financial modeling tools (Excel, custom calculators)

Deliverables:

  • Service area maps with home counts and density heat maps
  • Subscriber forecast models (5-year and 10-year)
  • Network capacity requirements
  • Service area prioritization and phasing recommendations

Phase 2: Network Architecture & PON Technology Selection

Topology Design:

  • Centralized Architecture ‚Äì Single central office (CO), suitable for serving areas within 20 km radius, lowest equipment cost
  • Distributed Architecture ‚Äì Multiple hub sites/cabinets, extends reach, reduces feeder fiber counts, higher equipment cost
  • Hybrid Architecture ‚Äì Combination approach balancing reach and cost, typical for mixed urban/suburban/rural areas

PON Technology Selection:

Technology Downstream Upstream Use Case
GPON 2.5 Gbps 1.25 Gbps Residential, legacy systems, cost-optimized
XGS-PON 10 Gbps 10 Gbps Multi-gigabit services, future-proof, symmetrical
NG-PON2 40+ Gbps 10+ Gbps Ultra-high capacity, wavelength multiplexing
Combo PON GPON + XGS-PON - Migration strategy, backward compatibility

Split Strategy:

  • Single-Stage Splitting ‚Äì One splitter location (1:32 or 1:64 typical), simplest design, best loss budget
  • Cascaded Splitting ‚Äì Two-stage (1:4 + 1:8 = 1:32), flexible drop terminal placement, enables gradual build-out
  • Centralized vs. Distributed Splits ‚Äì CO-based splitters (minimizes OSP), field splitters (reduces feeder fiber)

Deliverables:

  • Network architecture diagrams (logical and physical)
  • PON technology recommendation with justification
  • Split ratio strategy and splitter placement approach
  • Central office / hub site preliminary layouts

Phase 3: Optical Distribution Network (ODN) Engineering

Detailed fiber route design and optical budget engineering ensuring network performance within ITU-T specifications.

Fiber Route Design:

  • Feeder Network ‚Äì Central office to fiber distribution hub (FDH), high fiber count cables (144F to 864F), backbone infrastructure
  • Distribution Network ‚Äì FDH to network access points (NAPs), medium fiber count (12F to 144F), serves neighborhoods/clusters
  • Drop Network ‚Äì NAP to customer premises, low fiber count (1F, 2F, 4F), final connection to ONT

Route Optimization:

  • Aerial Routes ‚Äì Utility pole attachments, make-ready analysis, NESC clearance compliance, pole loading calculations
  • Underground Routes ‚Äì Conduit systems, duct banks, existing telecom infrastructure utilization, vault locations
  • Buried Routes ‚Äì Direct burial (rural areas), trenching vs. plowing, depth requirements (minimum 24-36 inches)
  • MDU Designs ‚Äì Riser cables, horizontal distribution, inside plant, demarcation points

Splitter Placement:

  • Fiber Distribution Hub (FDH) ‚Äì Pole-mounted or pedestal, primary splitter location (1:4, 1:8), serves multiple NAPs
  • Network Access Point (NAP) ‚Äì Secondary splitter location (1:4, 1:8), serves 4-16 homes, drop terminal interface
  • Centralized Splitters (CO) ‚Äì All splitting in central office, maximum flexibility, highest feeder fiber count

Phase 4: Optical Link Budget Engineering

Loss Budget Components:

  • Fiber Attenuation ‚Äì ITU-T G.652.D fiber: 0.35 dB/km @ 1310nm, 0.25 dB/km @ 1490nm, 0.22 dB/km @ 1550nm
  • Splitter Loss ‚Äì 1:2 (3.5 dB), 1:4 (7 dB), 1:8 (10.5 dB), 1:16 (14 dB), 1:32 (17.5 dB), 1:64 (21 dB)
  • Splice Loss ‚Äì Fusion splice: 0.05-0.1 dB per splice, mechanical splice: 0.1-0.3 dB
  • Connector Loss ‚Äì SC/APC, LC/APC connectors: 0.3-0.5 dB per mated pair

Link Budget Calculation Example (GPON, 1:32 Split, 20 km):

Design Criteria:

  • GPON ‚Äì Class B+ (28 dB budget), Class C+ (32 dB budget) for extended reach
  • XGS-PON ‚Äì N1 (29 dB), N2 (31 dB), E1 (33 dB), E2 (35 dB) optical budgets
  • Minimum Margin ‚Äì 2-3 dB after all losses and aging (ensures 20+ year operation)
  • Wavelength Coexistence ‚Äì GPON + XGS-PON on same fiber (1490/1577 nm downstream, 1310 nm upstream)

Deliverables:

  • Optical link budget spreadsheets (best/worst case scenarios)
  • Fiber route drawings with distances and fiber counts
  • Splitter location maps with configurations
  • Splice schedule and documentation templates
